Option C

Narrative Flow

The journey told as a story. Follow the line — see where it flows and where it breaks.

Procurement Data is the thread woven through every stage — 80K+ award decisions, the only signal no competitor has.
Acquire Fragmented

Someone visits tendium.com. Google Analytics sees a session. Segment fires an event. PostHog logs a pageview. But none of them know which company just showed up. Seven tools watch the door — nobody knows who walked in.

⚠ Visitor → prospect is a black box
Activate Best covered

Once they sign up, things click. PostHog tracks trial-to-paid conversion with real product usage data. Postmark measures whether they’re opening the tender digest — the first real value moment. This is Tendium’s cleanest stage.

✓ Fewest tools, clearest signal
Retain Mixed

PostHog sees usage drop. Postmark notices fewer opens. HubSpot logs a support ticket. Each system screams “churn risk” independently — but nobody triangulates the signals. A customer can go quiet across all three before anyone notices.

⚠ No unified churn signal
Revenue Clean

Younium holds the truth: 42M SEK ARR across 1,376 subscriptions. OneFlow manages contracts. Fortnox handles the books. The data is clean and trustworthy — but it lives in a vault, disconnected from the usage and engagement signals that predict expansion.

⚠ Revenue data isolated from usage
Referral Underutilized

NPS is migrating from HubSpot to PostHog. Response rates are low. There’s no referral program, no systematic way to turn happy customers into advocates. The customers winning 82% of their bids should be shouting from the rooftops — nobody’s asking them to.

⚠ No program, no tracking
