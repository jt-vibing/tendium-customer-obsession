Revenue & Financial Metrics 10
Annual Recurring Revenue ARR
Total annualized subscription revenue. The north-star financial metric. Calculated from Younium billing data; includes all active subscriptions.
Baseline: 42M SEK Younium
Monthly Recurring Revenue MRR
ARR divided by 12. Used for month-over-month tracking and billing cycles. Useful when comparing against monthly procurement payment data.
Younium
Average Contract Value ACV
Mean ARR per customer. Indicates pricing power and customer size mix. Higher ACV typically correlates with Scale plan.
~30.5K SEK Younium
Net Revenue Retention NRR
Percentage of revenue retained from existing customers after accounting for churn, downgrades, and expansion. >100% means existing customers grow faster than they leave.
110–120% Younium
B2G Revenue % btgRevenuePercent
Share of a company’s total revenue derived from government/public-sector contracts. A core ICP signal: sweet spot is 10–50%. Below = not dependent on procurement. Above = highly dependent but may not need Tendium.

Difference between a customer’s current ARR and the average ARR of peer companies with similar procurement volume and sector. A positive gap = customer is under-paying relative to their B2G activity.

Engagement & Activation 7
Weekly Active Companies WAC
Number of companies with at least one user engaging with Find in a given week. Replaces WAU (Weekly Active Users) because B2G SaaS engagement is company-level, not individual. The primary engagement metric.

How far a company has progressed through initial product adoption. “Fully activated” means they completed onboarding and use core features regularly. The activation moment for Tendium = first saved tender in Find.

Days from trial start to the activation moment (first saved tender). Shorter = better product onboarding. A leading indicator of trial-to-paid conversion.

Count of product interaction events (searches, saves, bid drafts, exports) per feature in the prior 30 days. Used to calculate usage trends.

Direction of engagement compared to prior period. Computed per feature (Find, BidFlow, Intelligence). A declining trend in core features is a churn predictor.

Product Tiers & Features 6
Light plan: light
Entry-level plan. Find only — intelligent search, AI summaries, documents/updates/awards, 1 tender filter. Self-service onboarding. Typically smaller companies or those evaluating B2G procurement for the first time.
Core plan: core
Mid-tier plan. Find + BidFlow — 5 tender filters, 1 BidFlow, collaboration, qualify, create bid, smart checklists, AI tasks, content library, Ask Tendium AI. Personal onboarding. Companies actively bidding on multiple tenders.
Scale plan: scale
Premium plan. Everything unlimited — unlimited tender filters, unlimited BidFlows, customizable BidFlow, user permissions, SSO, Call-Off Inbox. Custom onboarding. Typically 100+ employees with significant B2G revenue dependency.
Find
Core tender discovery module. Intelligent search, AI summaries, documents, updates, awards. Included in all plans. Saving a tender = the activation moment.
BidFlow
Team bid management workflow. Qualify, create bid, smart checklists, AI tasks, content library, project management. Available from Core plan (1 BidFlow), unlimited on Scale.
Intelligence add-on
Separate add-on product (not a plan tier). Expiring Contracts, Previous Procurements, Payment Data, Supplier Profiles, Buyer Profiles, Market Insights. Available for Core and Scale plans.

Health & Risk Scoring 6
Health Score
Composite 0–100 score reflecting overall account health. Inputs: usage trends, support ticket volume, NRR, procurement bid activity. ≥70 = green (healthy), 40–69 = yellow (attention), <40 = red (at risk).
0–100 Derived (multi-source)
Churn Risk
Probability of customer cancellation in the next renewal window. “High” churn risk compounds when multiple signals align: declining usage + approaching contract end + negative call sentiment.
low / medium / high
ICP Fit Score icpFitScore
How well a company matches Tendium’s Ideal Customer Profile. Based on: bid volume (>10/month), win rate (>25%), sectors matching ICP verticals, employee count, B2G revenue dependency (10–50%).
0–100 Derived (Procurement + Firmographic)
Net Promoter Score NPS
Customer satisfaction metric from surveys. Promoters (9–10) minus Detractors (0–6). A lagging indicator of account sentiment. Being transitioned from HubSpot to PostHog surveys.
−100 to +100 PostHog / HubSpot

Procurement bid volume as a leading external indicator of company health. A company whose bid volume is declining may be shrinking operationally — churn driven by business reality, not product friction. This distinction changes the CS response.

Business-to-Government. Tendium’s core market. Companies that sell products/services to public-sector organizations through formal procurement processes.

Percentage of procurement bids a company has won out of total bids submitted. High win rate (>40%) suggests strong bid capability; very high (>70%) may indicate niche dominance.

Data Quality Levels 5
Trusted trusted
High-confidence data from an authoritative source. Verified, current, and reliable for decision-making. Example: ARR from Younium billing system.
Directional directional
Approximate or incomplete data. Useful for spotting trends and relative comparisons, but not precise enough for exact calculations. Example: ICP fit score derived from partial firmographic data.
Unreliable unreliable
Known to be inconsistent, outdated, or from a low-confidence source. Display with caution and caveats. Should not drive automated actions.
Missing missing
Data not yet available or not collected for this entity. Display as “—” in the UI. Absence of data is itself a signal (e.g., no PostHog data = customer may not be using the product).
Ground Truth Principle
Always query raw source systems directly, never intermediary copies. No abstraction layers between the Compass and the source of truth. This ensures data freshness and eliminates sync drift.

AARRR Customer Journey 6
Acquire
How prospects discover Tendium. Currently fragmented across 8+ systems (website, GA, Segment, PostHog, HubSpot, Vainu, LinkedIn). Key gap: no company-level visitor identification. Procurement data enables Ghost TAM as an acquisition source.
Status: Fragmented
Activate
Trial-to-paid conversion. The activation moment = first saved tender in Find. Best-covered stage with PostHog (event tracking) and Postmark (tender digest engagement). Procurement data adds context: companies with high bid volume activate faster.
Status: Best covered
Retain
Keeping customers engaged and renewed. Tracked via PostHog (usage trends) and Postmark (digest engagement). Procurement data adds a business health signal: declining bid volume predicts external churn before product signals show it.
Status: Mixed
Revenue
Subscription revenue and expansion. Cleanest stage: Younium is the source of truth (42M SEK ARR). OneFlow for contracts, Fortnox for accounting. Procurement data powers upsell detection: growing bid volume + lower-tier plan = expansion opportunity.
Status: Cleanest
Referral
Customers referring new prospects. Currently underutilized: PostHog surveys (NPS) and HubSpot (legacy NPS). Zero current OKRs targeting referral. Company groups (Allabolag) enable warm referral paths through parent/sibling entities.
Status: Underutilized
Procurement Data Layer
Procurement data is the cross-cutting signal layer that spans all 5 AARRR stages. It provides unique signals at every stage: Ghost TAM (Acquire), activation prediction (Activate), business health (Retain), upsell detection (Revenue), and warm paths (Referral). No other data source spans all stages.

Intelligence Layer Concepts 9
Punch Card
AI-generated intelligence brief tailored to a specific role (Sales, CS, Leadership). Pre-computed from multiple data sources. The 7-star MVP experience — saves hours of manual data assembly into a 30-second read.
Sales Punch Card
6 sections: (1) Procurement Profile — bid history, sectors, win rates. (2) Current Relationship — plan, ARR, contract status. (3) Usage Snapshot — feature adoption, engagement trends. (4) Recent Activity — calls, awards, milestones. (5) Peer Comparison — benchmarks vs similar companies. (6) Talking Points — recommended conversation starters.
CS Health Brief
5 sections: (1) Health Traffic Light — score with explanation and trend. (2) Procurement Context — external business health signals. (3) Interaction Timeline — recent calls, emails, support. (4) Renewal Forecast — predicted outcome with confidence. (5) Suggested Check-in — AI-drafted message.
Leadership Monday Compass
Weekly executive brief. 5 sections: Path to 100M (ARR trajectory), WAC by Market (engagement scorecard), PCM Scorecard (paying customers by geography), Top 3 Risks, Top 3 Opportunities. Replaces Hugo’s 5-hour Monday spreadsheet assembly.
Speed Bar
The benchmark the Compass must beat: Hugo currently spends ~5 hours assembling customer data manually. Target: equivalent intelligence in 30 seconds. If the tool isn’t dramatically faster, adoption won’t happen.
5 hours → 30 seconds
0.1% Test
Defensibility check for intelligence features. Question: “Can this insight be replicated without Tendium’s proprietary data?” If yes, it’s commodity. If no (requires procurement data + customer data + usage data), it passes the 0.1% test and is defensible.
Expansion Readiness
A Tier 2 compound signal: bid volume growing + current plan is Light + peer companies at similar volume are on Scale = natural upsell opportunity. Only detectable by combining procurement data with subscription data.
Tier 2 intelligence
ICP Mismatch Alarm
A Tier 2 signal: high churn in a customer segment where procurement data shows low bid activity = the segment doesn’t need B2G tooling. Stop targeting it. A data-driven “don’t sell here” signal.
Tier 2 intelligence
MEDDIC / MEDDPICC
Enterprise sales qualification methodology. Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition. Procurement data directly fills the “M” (Metrics/Money) — proving quantifiable B2G value before the first sales call.
