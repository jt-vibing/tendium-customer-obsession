01 — Problem

Tendium Customer Graph

PRD & problem brief for building a unified internal customer intelligence layer at Tendium.

Problem

Core Pain
Every department at Tendium touches a different part of the same "elephant" — the customer — but nobody sees the whole animal. Definitions are confused, data is siloed across 9+ systems, and there's no shared source of truth.
CS Sales Marketing The Customer Product Leadership RevOps C.Ops
A — Definitions & Types
Sales says organic leads are 20% of pipeline. Marketing says 5%. Three different ARR figures depending on system.
B — Silos & Confusion
191 Garba calls analyzed in isolation. Hugo manually assembles data from 3+ systems weekly.
C — Sources of Data
9 systems, no join key. A customer in billing is a different entity than in product analytics.

These three problems are parallel. The Tendium Customer Graph is the forcing function for all three simultaneously.

Ideal Customer Profile

T
The Tendium Team Member
CS lead, Sales rep, Marketing ops, PM, or C-suite — anyone who needs to answer customer questions daily without building a spreadsheet
"I spend 2–4 hours every week pulling data from 3+ systems just to answer 'which accounts need attention right now?' — and half the time the numbers don't match."
  • ~30 people across departments making customer-facing decisions daily
  • Lost productivity: 30 × 3hrs × 46wks = 4,140 hrs/yr (~2M SEK wasted)
  • Real cost: wrong decisions on partial data at a company targeting 100M SEK

Solution

An AI-first internal customer compass — a unified entity model that stitches together Procurement Data (#1), PostHog (#2), and Younium (#3) into a single company-level view, accessible through department-specific lenses.

Falsifiable Hypothesis
If we give every team a single source of customer truth with department-specific views and agreed-upon definitions, teams will make daily decisions from the graph — because 10 seconds beats 30 minutes of manual assembly.

Features — V1 MVP

Entity View
One company, one card. Five department lenses. Every metric shows source + quality level.
Procurement Intel
Bid history, win rates, payment volumes, sector overlap from procurement data.
Quality Signals
Green (trusted), yellow (directional), red (unreliable), grey (missing). Forces transparency.
Definition Negotiation
Shared definitions with agreed/proposed/disputed status. Alignment before numbers.

Constraints

Constraint Type Why
6-week sprint (ends ~March 20) Hard Advisory engagement has fixed end date.
Procurement Data as primary source Hard Covers customers AND non-customers.
No HubSpot centralization Hard Three failed attempts. Team has rejected it.
Mobile-friendly web app Hard Must work in meetings, on phones.
Sell/Build data boundary Hard Customer data powers GTM. Product data powers platform. The overlap is the insight.
3 data sources max initially Soft Procurement + PostHog + Younium.
PostHog tracking incomplete Soft Usage data is directional, not precise.

Non-Goals

  • Not a dashboard (entity-first, not chart-first)
  • Not a HubSpot replacement (not building a CRM)
  • Not a data warehouse (stitches views at query time)
  • Not a product feature (internal tooling only, Sell vs Build boundary)
  • Not a WAU counter (company-level value, not individual logins)
Success =
By March 6 (Week 4), at least 3 department leads independently open the customer compass to answer a real question about a real account — AND we can report PCM for at least 2 markets.
